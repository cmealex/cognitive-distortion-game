Cognitive Distortions Guide

All-or-Nothing Thinking
Seeing things in black-and-white categories. If your performance falls short of perfect, you see yourself as a total failure.
"I got a B on the test. I'm a complete failure."
Overgeneralization
Seeing a single negative event as a never-ending pattern of defeat.
"He didn't call me back. No one will ever love me."
Mental Filter
Picking out a single negative detail and dwelling on it exclusively.
"The meal was ruined because the dessert was overcooked."
Disqualifying the Positive
Rejecting positive experiences by insisting they "don't count."
"That compliment doesn't count because they were just being nice."
